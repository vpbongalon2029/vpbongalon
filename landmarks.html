Landmarks & Tourist Attractions

Experience the beauty from within

Taal Volcano and Lake

Tagaytay’s biggest asset is this ancient volcano. Part of what makes it special is not what’s within its territory but what one can admire from there — the Taal Lake and the picturesque but temperamental volcano at its center.

Unbeknownst to many, what we now call Taal Lake is actually the caldera of a massive ancient volcano. Only the tiny island in the middle remains active, explaining Tagaytay’s unique geography and topography.

Sky Ranch

This five-hectare leisure park caters to both kids and kids-at-heart. It features rides, restaurants, horseback riding, and provides good views of Taal Volcano.

Tagaytay Picnic Grove

Aside from picnic areas, it features eco-trails, horseback riding circuits, open fields for kite flying, zipline, souvenir shops, and food stalls.

Kawa Hot Bath

A “kawa” is an oversized metal vat traditionally used for cooking large feasts. Now you can relax in these and melt your worries away.

Praying Hands

The "Praying Hands" sculpture reflects Tagaytay's deep faith and core values, serving as a symbolic artwork for future generations.

Our Lady of Manaoag

Walking about 500 meters from Picnic Grove, a towering 50-meter red image of the Virgin Mary captures attention. It's a replica of Pangasinan's Our Lady of Manaoag.
